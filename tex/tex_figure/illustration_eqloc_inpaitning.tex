                \caption{Experimental results illustrate the behavior of local and equivariant estimators for the inpainting task. Outside the kernel of $A$, both the physics-agnostic ($B=\Id$) and physics-informed ($B=A^+$, which equals $A$ since $A$ is a diagonal matrix with $0$ and $1$ entries on the diagonal) estimators behave almost identically. The key advantage of the physics-informed approach lies inside the kernel of $A^+$. There, the estimator makes a "safe" decision by averaging over all pixels from $\D$ that lie within the kernel, whereas the physics-agnostic estimator ($B=\Id$) produces inconsistent, noise-driven estimates. Consequently, for the inpainting task, the physics-informed solver achieves superior performance.}
